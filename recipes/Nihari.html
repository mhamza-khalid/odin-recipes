Lamb Nihari

Description

Lamb Nihari is a rich and aromatic slow-cooked meat stew originating from the Indian subcontinent. It is a traditional dish enjoyed for its tender and flavorful pieces of lamb, immersed in a velvety, spiced gravy. Nihari is often served with naan bread or rice, making it a satisfying and comforting meal.

Ingredients

  • 1 kg lamb meat, bone-in
  • 2 onions, finely sliced
  • 4 tablespoons ghee or cooking oil
  • 2 tablespoons ginger paste
  • 2 tablespoons garlic paste
  • 2 tablespoons wheat flour (optional, for thickening)
  • 2 teaspoons red chili powder
  • 2 teaspoons turmeric powder
  • 2 teaspoons coriander powder
  • 2 teaspoons garam masala powder
  • Salt to taste
  • 6 cups water
  • Fresh ginger, thinly sliced (for garnish)
  • Fresh coriander leaves, chopped (for garnish)

Recipe

  1. Heat ghee or oil in a large, heavy-bottomed pot or Dutch oven.
  2. Add the sliced onions and cook until golden brown.
  3. Add ginger paste and garlic paste. Saut√© for a minute.
  4. Add lamb meat to the pot and cook until it changes color.
  5. In a separate bowl, mix wheat flour with a little water to make a paste (if using). Add this paste to the pot and cook for a few minutes.
  6. Add red chili powder, turmeric powder, coriander powder, garam masala powder, and salt. Stir well to coat the meat evenly with the spices.
  7. Pour in water and bring the mixture to a boil.
  8. Reduce the heat to low, cover the pot, and let it simmer for 4-5 hours until the meat is tender and the flavors are well infused. Stir occasionally.
  9. Once the meat is cooked, increase the heat and cook uncovered for another 15-20 minutes to thicken the gravy.
  10. Garnish with fresh ginger slices and chopped coriander leaves.
  11. Serve hot with naan bread or steamed rice.
